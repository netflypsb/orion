# Comprehensive List of Relevant Papers: Phase 2 Literature Search

This document lists 20 highly relevant papers (2019-2025) identified through enhanced searches using Scholar, Tavily (advanced), and Serp Google. Focus: AI/ML integration with hyperspectral/LiDAR on drones/UAVs for haze/aerosol/PM2.5 monitoring and carbon/emission/sequestration estimation in Malaysia/SE Asia. Prioritized recent, open-access where possible. Formatted in APA style. Includes links; full-text URLs noted.

## 1. Gohari, A., Ahmad, A. B., Rabiu, L., et al. (2024). *A systematic review of the UAV technology usage in ASEAN*. IEEE Open Journal of the Communications Society. https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/10616173/  
   - Relevance: Reviews UAVs for transboundary haze pollution in ASEAN; discusses LiDAR, multispectral/hyperspectral sensors.  
   - Full-text: Paywall (IEEE).

## 2. Saengpradit, P., Sangpradid, S., & Laosuwan, T. (2024). *Estimation of above-ground biomass using hybrid machine learning based on satellite imagery*. International Journal of Technology and Physics Education, 16(4), 7-13. https://www.iotpe.com/IJTPE/IJTPE-2024/IJTPE-Issue61-Vol16-No4-Dec2024/2-IJTPE-Issue61-Vol16-No4-Dec2024-pp7-13.pdf  
   - Relevance: ML for AGB/carbon sequestration; mentions LiDAR/hyperspectral integration in tropical SE Asia.  
   - Full-text: Open access PDF (direct link).

## 3. Mohan, M. (2024). *Evaluating climate, environmental and anthropogenic influences on regenerating tropical and mangrove forests through remote sensing: Insights into nature-based solutions*. ProQuest Dissertations. https://search.proquest.com/openview/eceaeeb054238592ce2b17787c373b4c/1?pq-origsite=gscholar&cbl=18750  
   - Relevance: Hyperspectral, SAR, LiDAR for forest carbon; UAV-LiDAR ML in tropics.  
   - Full-text: Partial; full via ProQuest.

## 4. Caron, N., Noura, H. N., Nakache, L., Guyeux, C., & Aynes, B. (2025). *AI for wildfire management: From prediction to detection, simulation, and impact analysis—Bridging lab metrics and real-world validation*. AI, 6(10), 253. https://www.mdpi.com/2673-2688/6/10/253  
   - Relevance: AI/ML with drones for wildfire/haze; forest monitoring in SE Asia contexts.  
   - Full-text: Open access (MDPI).

## 5. Bahadur, F. T., Shah, S. R., & Nidamanuri, R. R. (2023). *Applications of remote sensing vis-à-vis machine learning in air quality monitoring and modelling: A review*. Environmental Monitoring and Assessment, 195(12), 1-25. https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10661-023-12001-2  
   - Relevance: RS + ML for air quality (CO, PM); hyperspectral applications.  
   - Full-text: Paywall (Springer).

## 6. Neo, E. X., Hasikin, K., Lai, K. W., Mokhtar, M. I., et al. (2023). *Artificial intelligence-assisted air quality monitoring for smart city management*. PeerJ Computer Science, 9, e1306. https://peerj.com/articles/cs-1306/

   - Full-text: Open access (PeerJ).

## 7. Kamaludeen, A. M., Yusoff, I. M., & Yusuf, S. (2024). *A review: Identifying and analyzing air pollution hotspots using machine learning and remote sensing techniques*. International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health. https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Muhammad-Kamaludeen-Adamu/publication/396442344_A_Review_Identifying_and_Analyzing_Air_Pollution_Hotspots_Using_Machine_Learning_and_Remote_Sensing_Techniques/links/68ebca3f220a341aa154b0d8/A-Review-Identifying-and-Analyzing-Air-Pollution-Hotspots-Using-Machine-Learning-and-Remote-Sensing-Techniques.pdf
